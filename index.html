Workshop on Mathematical Foundations of Machine Learning

Exploring the theoretical underpinnings and future directions of ML

4th - 5th July, 2026

Peking University, China

Register Now

In today's data-driven world, machine learning plays an increasingly pivotal role, but with this prominence comes the need for rigorous mathematical underpinnings to ensure trustworthiness. There has been a recent flurry of theoretical research activities that examine fundamental principles such as approximation, generalization, optimization, mathematical foundations of LLMs and other societal aspects of machine learning algorithms.

Our aim is to create a forum where researchers at all career stages can engage in a vibrant exchange of ideas, foster collaborations, identify pressing challenges, and collectively advance the mathematical foundations of deep learning. This workshop will bring together a unique diverse mix of leading researchers from (i) different fields (such as theoretical computer science, statistics, optimization, causality), (ii) different parts of the world (in Europe, North America, and Asia), and (iii) a balanced group of junior and senior researchers. The planned session topics will include:

  • Theory of deep learning - Approximation viewpoint
  • Theory of deep learning - stat. / generalization viewpoint
  • Theory of deep learning - optimization viewpoint
  • Statistical physics for deep learning
  • Mathematical foundations for LLMs
